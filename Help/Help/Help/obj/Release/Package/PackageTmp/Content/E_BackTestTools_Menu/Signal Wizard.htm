Signal Wizard

Signal Backtest trading models use indicators such as RSI, MACD, moving averages as the criteria for entering and exiting a trade.

 

In this section, we are going to show you how to perform a BackTest in the Signal Modeling Wizard by using an example:

  • We are going to BackTest a Bullish strategy for NFLX over the past 10 years. 
  • We are going to purchase one long call every time the RSI(5) crosses below 20 and exit the trade when the RSI(5) crosses above 60.
  • The long call will have the following characteristics:
  • Days to expiration: minimum of 30 days to maximum of 70 days
  • Strike Selection: 40-60 Delta Range
  • Profit target: 25% of the Initial Cost
  • Stop loss: 50% of the Initial Cost

There are 5 steps to complete this BackTest: Entry Signal >> Exit Signal >> Entry-Exit Dates >> Trade Rules >> Preview

Entry Signal:

  1. Input a name for the signal to be saved under RSI(5) cross below 20. 
  2. Select the RSI indicator and fill in the Days as 5, Operator as Cross below and Value as 20 then click to close and save.
  3. Click to save your Entry Signal and to move on to Exit Signal.

Exit Signal:

  1. Input a name for the signal to be saved under RSI(5) cross above 60. 
  2. Select the RSI indicator and fill in the Days as 5, Operator as Cross above and Value as 60 then click to close and save.
  3. Click to save your Exit Signal and to move on to Entry-Exit Dates.

Entry-Exit Dates:

  1. Type in a name for the dates to be saved under NFLX Bullish RSI(5). 
  2. Select the stock you want to test NFLX. 
  3. Enter the date range to be tested in From Date as 01/03/2007 and To Date as 05/08/2017.
  4. Select the entry signal as RSI(5) cross below 20 (created in Entry) and exit signal as RSI(5) cross above 60 (created in Exit).
  5. Click to save your Entry-Exit Dates and to move on to Trade Rules.

Trade Rules:

  1. Type a name for the trade rules to be saved under NFLX Long Call. 
  2. Click the Strategy drop down menu and select Long Call.
  1. Input the minimum and maximum days to expiration as 30 and 70.
  2. Input the minimum and maximum delta range as 40 and 60.
  3. Input the profit target % as 25 based off the InitialCost.
  4. Input the stop loss % as 50 based off the InitialCost.
  5. Click to save your Trade Rules and to move on to Preview.

Preview:

All fields will auto-populate.  For risk per trade, you can select a dollar amount/trade, contracts/trade or a custom amount created manually.
