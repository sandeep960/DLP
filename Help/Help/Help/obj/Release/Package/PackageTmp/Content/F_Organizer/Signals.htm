Signals

Signals are used to create a filter based on certain criteria for symbols to be traded. Users can create Signals with the list of different criteria and trade options based on those criteria.

Each criteria is described with examples in this section.

STOCK FILTER

Price

This signal filters the symbols that have prices between minimum and maximum values, an increasing or decreases price trend or a selectable amount of consecutive up or down days.

 

 

Price % Change

This signal filters for symbols that have certain % change over a certain period of days and in the direction you are looking for.

 

 

Stock Volume

This signal filters symbols that match volume requirements for average daily volume, relative volume, directional trending volume or directional consecutive volume.

 

 

Gap Up

This signal filters for symbols that open a certain percentage higher than its previous day’s high price.

 

 

Gap Down

This signal filters for symbols that open a certain percentage lower than its previous day’s low price.

 

 

Earnings

This signal filters for symbols that have a specific type of trading active during earnings periods.

  Seasonal Price YearlyThis signal generates a list of symbols based on user defined criteria for upcoming time period, required return and number of occurrences over the last 10 years.
 

 

Sectors

This signals filters for symbols to match the selected sector and industry.

  SplitThis signal filters signals with a procedure that increases or decreases a corporation's total number of shares outstanding without altering the company's market value or the proportionate ownership interest of existing shareholders.
  AssetThis signals filters for symbols to match the selected asset.
  OHLCThis signal filters for symbols based on open, high, low and close criteria entered by the user.
  Earnings Day

This signal filters for symbols that are a specified number of days ahead of their earnings announcement.

 

 

INDICATORS

EMA

This signal filters for symbols that match the specified EMA criteria entered by the user.

 

 

SMA

This signal filters for symbols based on specified SMA criteria entered by the user.

 

 

ATR

This signal filters for symbols based on the specified ATR criteria entered by the user.

 

 

ATRJWW

This signal filters for symbols based on the specified ATRJWW criteria entered by the user.

 

 

New High

This signal filters for symbols that make a new high over the past certain number of days.

 

 

New Low

This signal filters for symbols that make a new low over the past certain number of days.

  BB (Bollinger Bands)
 

 

Aroon Up

This signal filters for symbols based on Aroon up criteria entered by the user.

 

 

Aroon Down

This signal filters for symbols based on Aroon down criteria entered by the user.

 

 

Aroon Oscillator

This signal filters for symbols based on Aroon oscillator criteria entered by the user.

  Statistic VolatilityStatistical volatility is any measure of volatility derived from the prices and price movements of the asset itself. This signal is calculated using the short days divided by the long days to produce a ratio that indicates relative volatility.
 

 

RSI

 

This signal is the Relative Strength Index (RSI) that measures the speed and change of price movements.

  High-Low Band This signal filters symbols with a range of percentage retracements from a specific high or low price.
  ADXThe average directional index (ADX) signal is used to measure the strength of a trend.
  +DIThis signal is a component of the ADX that is used to measure the presence of an uptrend. When the +DI is sloping upward, it signals that the uptrend is getting stronger.
  -DI-DI is a component of the ADX that is used to measure the presence of a downtrend. When the -DI is sloping upward, it signals that the downtrend is getting stronger.
  MACDThe Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) is used by traders to determine when to buy or sell a security, based on the interaction between a line constructed from two moving averages and a "signal line."
  MACD HistogramAs with MACD signal, the MACD-Histogram signal is also designed to identify convergence, divergence and crossovers. The MACD-Histogram measures the distance between main line and the signal line.
  Beta

This signal is a measure of the risk potential of a stock or an investment portfolio expressed as a ratio of the stock's or portfolio's volatility to the volatility of the market as a whole.

 

 

STATE MODELING

State Model

This signal filters for symbols based on State Modeling™ technique.

  Next StateThis signal allows users to find symbols based on their current state and their next highest probable state.
  State PathThis signal allows users to find symbols based on their current state and previous two states (State Path).
  State Targets

This signal allows users to find symbols based on their current state and whether or not their targets or stops losses have be met.

 

OPTION FILTERS

Put/Call Ratio

This signal filters for symbols based on put/call ratio criteria entered by the user.

 

 

Open Interest

This signal filters for symbols based on open interest criteria entered by the user.

 

 

IV Rank

This signal filters for symbols based on IV rank criteria entered by the user.

 

 

Option Volume

This signal provides the total number of symbols contracts traded on header date. This value is reset to zero every trading day.

 

 

Option Type

The Option Type lets you select symbols that are traded weekly or monthly.

 

 

Option Spread

Option Spread finds the average (ask-bid) liquidity from all symbols.

 

 

Implied Volatility

This signal filters for symbols based on IV criteria entered by the user.

 

 

SIGNAL

Time Stop

This signal filters for symbols based on number of time stop days.
