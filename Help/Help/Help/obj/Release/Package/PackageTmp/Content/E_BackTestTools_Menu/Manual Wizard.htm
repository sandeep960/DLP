Manual Wizard

The Manual Wizard backtests models by manually inputting dates for your stocks or stock list.

 

In this section, we are going to show you how to perform a BackTest in the Manual Modeling Wizard by using an example:

  • We are going to BackTest a Bearish strategy for SPX. 
  • We are going to purchase one put for SPX on the first trading day of May for the past ten years.  We will exit the trade on the first trading day of August. 
  • The long put will have the following characteristics:
  • Days to expiration: minimum of 90 days to maximum of 120 days
  • Strike Selection: 40-60 Delta Range
  • Profit target: 150% of the Initial Cost
  • Stop loss: 75% of the Initial Cost

 

There are 3 steps to complete this BackTest: Entry-Exit Dates -> Trade Rules -> Preview

Entry-Exit Dates:

  1. Input a name for the dates to be saved under Sell in May_SPX. 
  2. Input the stock you want to test in the Symbol column. 
  3. Input the dates to open the trade in the Entry Date column.
  4. Enter the dates to close the trade in the Exit Date column.
  5. Click if you want to add more rows and trades.
  6. Click to save your Entry-Exit Dates and to move on to Trade Rules.

Trade Rules:

  1. Input a name for the trade rules to be saved under Long Put. 
  2. Click the Strategy drop down menu and select Long Put.
  1. Enter the minimum and maximum days to expiration as 90 and 120.
  2. Input the minimum and maximum delta range as 40 and 60.
  3. Input the profit target % as 150 based off the InitialCost.
  4. Enter the stop loss % as 75 based off the InitialCost.
  5. Click to save your Trade Rules and to move on to Preview.

Preview:

All fields will auto-populate.  For risk per trade, you can select a dollar amount/trade, contracts/trade or a custom amount created manually.

  1. Click to have the system run the tests.
  2. Select to go to the BackTest results page.
