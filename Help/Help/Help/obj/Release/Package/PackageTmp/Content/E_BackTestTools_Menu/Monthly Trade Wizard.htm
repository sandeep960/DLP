Monthly Trade Wizard

Monthly Trades Backtest is designed to help you design a strategy based on systematic calendar trading with monthly options expirations.

Backtest using monthly calendar trading strategies such as Iron Condors and variations of butterflies as a way to generate income.

 

In this section, we are going to show you how to perform a BackTest in the Monthly Trade Modeling Wizard by using an example:

  • We are going to conduct a BackTest for a monthly Iron Condor strategy on SPY from 01/03/2007.
  • We are going to open an Iron Condor one day after the previous month’s expiration and close the trade one day before the current expiration.
  • The Iron Condor will have the following characteristics:

  • Days to expiration: minimum of 5 days to maximum of 35 days

  • Strike Selection :

    • Long Call: 25-40 Delta range

    • Minimum width between calls: $2
    • Short Call: 30-45 Delta range
    • Minimum Distance between shorts: $5
    • Short Put: 30-45 Delta range
    • Minimum width between puts: $2
    • Long Put: 25-40 Delta range

  • Profit target: 100% of maximum profit

  • Stop loss: 25% of maximum risk

There are 3 steps to complete this BackTest: Entry-Exit Dates >> Trade Rules >> Preview

Entry-Exit Dates:

  1. Input a name for the dates to be saved under SPY Iron Condor. 
  2. Input the stock symbol as SPY. 
  3. Input the date range to be tested in From Date 01/03/2007 and To Date 05/07/2017.
  4. For trade entry select Days after Monthly Expiration and Number of Days as 1.
  5. For trade exit select Days before Monthly Expiration and input Number of Days as 1 and Skip as 1 monthly expiration.
  6. Click to save your Entry-Exit Dates and to move on to Trade Rules.

Trade Rules:

  1. Input a name for the trade rules to be saved under SPY IC. 
  2. Click the Strategy drop down menu and select Iron Condor.
  1. Input the minimum and maximum days to expiration as 5 and 35.
  2. Input the minimum and maximum delta ranges:
  • Long Call (Buy1) range 25 and 40
  • Short Call (Sell1) range 30 and 45
  • Long Put (Buy2) range 25 and 40
  • Short Put (Sell2) range 30 and 45
  1. Input the minimum distance between the short legs as 5; the minimum width between the calls as 2; and the minimum width between the puts as 2.
  2. Input the profit target % as 100 based off the Max Profit.
  3. Input the stop loss % as 25 based off the Max Risk.
  4. Click to save your Entry-Exit Dates and to move on to Preview.

Preview:

All fields will auto-populate.  For risk per trade, you can select a dollar amount/trade, contracts/trade or a custom amount created manually.
