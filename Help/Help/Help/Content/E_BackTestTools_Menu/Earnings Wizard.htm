Earnings Wizard

The Earnings Backtest is designed to help you design a strategy to trade options around the earnings of the underlying.

  1. Stocks tend to rise or fall significantly after an earnings report.
  2. Implied Volatility expands and contracts around the earnings report.
  3. There are five different kinds of earnings strategies to use that help you trade around the price movement and volatility rise/fall around earnings day.

 

In this section, we are going to show you how to perform a BackTest in the Earnings Wizard by using an example:

  • We are going to BackTest a Bullish strategy for Twitter’s past 10 earnings reports. 
  • We are going to purchase one long call after TWTR’s earnings report and run BackTests to exit the trade 10, 15 & 20 days later.
  • The long call will have the following characteristics:
  • Days to expiration: minimum of 30 days to maximum of 60 days
  • Strike Selection: 40-60 Delta Range
  • Profit target: 50% of the Initial Cost
  • Stop loss: 25% of the Initial Cost

 

There are 3 steps to complete this BackTest: Entry-Exit Dates >> Trade Rules >> Preview.

Entry-Exit Dates:

  1. Input the stock symbol TWTR. 
  2. Input a name for the dates to be saved under TWTR Runner. 
  3. Select the type of Earning Play as Runner.
  4. De-select any Earnings Date(s) you want to exclude from the BackTest results.

Trade Rules:

  1. Input a name for the trade rules to be saved under TWTR Long Call. 

  2. Click the strategy drop down menu and select Long Call.

  3. Input the minimum and maximum days to expiration 30 and 60.
  4. Input the minimum and maximum delta range 40 and 60.
  5. Input the profit target % as 50 based off the InitialCost.
  6. Input the stop loss % as 25 based off the InitialCost.
  7. Click to save your Trade Rules and to move on to Preview.

Preview:

All fields will auto-populate.  For risk per trade, you can select a dollar amount/trade, contracts/trade or a custom amount created manually.
