Risk Per Trade

In trading, one should risk only the amount that he or she is willing to lose. There will be multiple opportunities to trade. Money management is an essential part of successful trading strategy. Diversifying strategies or investment vehicles will help mitigate some market or system risks.

Risk tolerance levels vary from person to person. When traders take positions, reward expectation should be higher than risk-free rate for successful investment strategy.

 

Some risks are listed below for understanding investment risks but they cover only few risks associated with investments. They can be broadly divided as:

  • System Risk - It covers undesired impact due to macroeconomics, social, geopolitics, global exposures and others.
  • Market Risk - It covers undesired impact due to business cycles in overall market.
  • Industry Risk – It covers the whole industry that the firm is doing business with might be facing some common issues: low supply in raw materials, increase in regulations and others.
  • Equity Risk - This risk is directly associated with the firm and how well the firm is able to meet or exceed the expectations and how well the firm is prepared to mitigate the risks associated strategically or operationally.
  • Trading Risk: This can be defined as how much the investor is willing to lose on any given trade.

 

The Risk Per Trade page defines the trading risk.

When you go LONG or SHORT stock, the whole amount invested in the stock becomes absolute risk.

When you trade using options strategies, the trading risk differs from one strategy to another. However, broadly, there are two types of options strategies - Debit & Credit strategies.

 

The Risk Per Trade page allows the user to create a name for the trade, the amount the user is investing, the risk percentage amount per trade, minimum options contracts the user plans to trade and the minimum shares.

Add new Risk Per Trade

To add a new Risk Per Trade, click on the Risk Per Trade Page.

 

When you click , the following page is displayed:

 

The page has the following fields:

 

Risk Per Trade

Name

It is a text-field to input the trade name.
 

 

Amount (in $)

It is a text-field to input the total trading dollars you allocate for trading options or stocks.

 
 

 

Risk % Amount (Per Trade) Debit Strategies

It is a text-field to input a % of the overall amount that will be under risk for each trade opportunity.

 
 

 

Risk % Amount (Per Trade) Credit Strategies

It is a text-field to input a % of the overall amount that will be under risk for each trade opportunity.

 
 

 

Minimum Option Contracts

If the entered Risk % Amount brings back less than 1 contract, technically you cannot open a trade in that opportunity.

 

The value in this field will be used as number of contracts, if calculated contracts is less than this value irrespective of the risk amount allocated in Risk % field or Amount (in $) as well.

 
 

 

Minimum Shares

If the entered Risk % Amount brings back less than 1 share, technically you cannot open a trade in that opportunity.

 

The value in this field will be used as number of shares, if calculated shares is less than this value irrespective of the risk amount allocated in Risk % field or Amount (in $) as well.

 
 

 

Using buying Power reduction

In trading strategies like short stock or credit strategies, broker holds a certain % of risk as initial margin money based on the type of the ticker you are trading and type of account combined with guidelines from exchanges of SEC. When your loss exceeds the hold amount, there will be margin call, you are expected to deposit more funds within the stipulated time, and they have the right to sell the position in order to reduce their exposure.

 

If this field is selected as “Yes”, the number of contracts will be calculated based on margin money instead of absolute risk.

Delete a Risk Per Trade

To delete a Risk Per Trade:

Select any row and click to delete the Risk Per Trade.

 

A. Where is Risk Per Trade used?

In the BackTest option, the defined values will be used to determine the number of shares or contracts that needs to be opened in a position.

 

B. Can I save a default Risk per trade for every back test I do.

In the General Settings under the User Profile, you can select the Risk Per Trade in the field Default Risk per trade (BackTest) to be used as a default value for every trade rule that is created.

 

C. Can I save a default Risk Per Trade for live trades?

In the General Settings under the User Profile, you can select the Risk Per Trade in the field Risk per trade (Live Trading) to be used as default value for every trade in brokerage order that is created.
