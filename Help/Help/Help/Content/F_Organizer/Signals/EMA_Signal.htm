EMA Signal

An Exponential Moving Average (EMA) is a type of moving average that is similar to a simple moving average, except that the most recent data gets the greatest weight and each price value gets a smaller weight as we go back in the series chronologically.

 

Examples:

Open/High/Low/Close: If you want to find symbols that have closing price greater than the 20 day EMA: select Close as Field, select > as Operator, and select 20 as Days 1.

EMA: If you want to find symbols with their 10 day EMA less than their 20 day EMA: select Field=EMA, Operator=<, EMA Days=10 and EMA Days=20.

SMA: If you want to find symbols with their 50 day EMA crossed above their 200 day SMA: select Field=SMA, Operator=Bullish Cross, EMA Days=50 and SMA Days=200.
